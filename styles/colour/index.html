Colour

Colour plays an important role in creating a consistent and strong visual identify for NSW Government digital products and services.

On this page

  • Colour usage
  • Design System base colour palette
  • NSW Government wide colour palette

Colour usage

The following guidance explores the use and application of colour in the NSW Design System for Level 01, Level 02 and Level 03 colour usage classifications.

For information about NSW Government branding and colour usage classifications please see the [NSW Government brand framework].

Design System base colour palette

The Design System base colour palette reflects Level 01 colour usage, which applies to Masterbrand Level 1 and 2 brand classifications. It uses Blues, Reds and Greys from the NSW Government wider colour palette to communicate a strong association with NSW Government.

The Design Systemâ€™s built in framework operationalise colour usage and inheritance to ensure a consistent and accessible user experience. Guidance on further theming options are available for Designing and Developing.

Text colours

This set of text colours have a ratio of at least 4.5:1 (or 3:1 for text larger than 18 point or at least 14 point when bolded) to met Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG 2.1).

NSW Government wider colour palette

The NSW Government wider colour palette has been developed to accommodate the wide range of expression required by our brand. Level 02 and 03 colour usage has access to the full colour palette for all Brand colours to allow for greater expression and flexibility where needed.

Guidance to adapted the Design System base colour palette for Level 02 and 03 colour usage is available for Designing and Developing.
